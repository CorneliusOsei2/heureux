## **Q1**

### **Passage**

```
Si tu veux, je t'emmène à la piscine cet après-midi. Qu'est-ce que
tu en penses ?
(If you want, I'll take you to the pool this afternoon. What do you
think?)
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | (Réponse à la proposition) | (Response to the suggestion) |
| A | J'ai cassé ma raquette | I broke my racket |
| B | J'ai oublié mon ballon | I forgot my ball |
| **C** | **J'ai perdu mon maillot** | **I lost my swimsuit** |
| D | J'ai trouvé mes chaussures | I found my shoes |

### **Correct Answer: C -- J'ai perdu mon maillot**

"La piscine" = swimming pool. You need a "maillot" (swimsuit) to swim. "J'ai perdu mon maillot" (I lost my swimsuit) is the only response logically connected to a pool invitation -- it explains why going might be a problem.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- J'ai cassé ma raquette.** A racket is for tennis or badminton, not swimming. No link to the pool.

**B -- J'ai oublié mon ballon.** A ball is irrelevant to swimming. Forgetting one wouldn't prevent going to the pool.

**D -- J'ai trouvé mes chaussures.** Finding shoes has zero connection to accepting or declining a pool invitation.
