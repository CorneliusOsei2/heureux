### **Q2 Dialogue (for reference)**

```
-- Sophie, dis-moi pourquoi tu es toujours aussi en
forme ?

histoires drôles. Je regarde le sport à la télé et je
vais au cinéma.

watch sports on TV and I go to the cinema.)
```

Only "je fais souvent des exercices" (I often do exercises) relates to physical fitness. The others — reading, watching TV, going to the cinema — are leisure activities, not fitness.

| **Prompt** | En Afrique, quelle tendance suit la littérature destinée à la jeunesse ? | In Africa, what trend does youth literature follow? |
| **A** | **Elle tend à devenir une forme d'expression spécifique** | **It tends to become a specific form of expression** |
| B | Elle reste cantonnée aux frontières du continent africain | It remains confined to the African continent's borders |
| C | Elle réunit plusieurs générations au sein d'un même lectorat | It brings together several generations within a single readership |
| D | Elle soutient la popularité du français auprès des enfants | It supports the popularity of French among children |

### **Correct Answer: A -- Elle tend à devenir une forme d'expression spécifique**

"En prenant de l'ampleur, commençait à avoir une identité propre" (as it grew, began to have its own identity) = youth literature in Africa is evolving from general literature into its own specific form of expression.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**B -- Cantonnée aux frontières.** No geographic limitation is discussed. The focus is on literary identity, not reach.

**C -- Réunit plusieurs générations.** No mention of cross-generational readership. The trend is about differentiation from general literature.

**D -- Popularité du français.** While the context involves French-language African writing, the trend discussed is about children's literature developing its own IDENTITY, not about French language popularity.
